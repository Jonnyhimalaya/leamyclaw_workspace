## Scoring Model
Score each opportunity from **1 to 5** on each axis.

### 1. Frequency
How often does this workflow happen?
- 1 = rare
- 3 = weekly
- 5 = daily / many times per day

### 2. Pain
How frustrating / costly is it today?
- 1 = minor annoyance
- 3 = noticeable drag
- 5 = severe bottleneck / regularly causes problems

### 3. Structure
How structured is the workflow?
- 1 = highly ambiguous / messy
- 3 = mixed
- 5 = clear inputs, steps, outputs

### 4. Risk
What happens if AI gets it wrong?
- 1 = high risk / serious downside
- 3 = manageable if supervised
- 5 = low risk / easy to verify

### 5. Leverage
How much value would solving this create?
- 1 = low impact
- 3 = helpful
- 5 = major time / money / customer experience gain

### 6. Integration Feasibility
How easy is it to connect to the tools/process needed?
- 1 = blocked / unclear / fragile
- 3 = possible with some friction
- 5 = straightforward

## Interpretation
### 24–30
**Excellent pilot candidate**
- likely structured
- high frequency / high pain
- meaningful upside
- safe enough to start with

### 18–23
**Strong candidate**
- valuable, but may need tighter approval or a more careful rollout

### 12–17
**Possible later**
- maybe worth doing after trust, data quality, or integration readiness improves

### <12
**Not a good early target**
- too risky, too vague, too low-value, or too hard to integrate

---

## Candidate Categories
After scoring, classify each opportunity:

### A. Build now
High score, low-to-medium risk, strong leverage

### B. Draft/recommend only
Useful, but needs human approval built in

### C. Observe first
Needs monitoring/data visibility before we let AI act

### D. Avoid for now
Too risky, too messy, or too little value

---

## Decision Questions
After scoring, ask:
- Which workflow has the best balance of value and safety?
- Which one will the client *feel* immediately?
- Which one is easiest to explain and prove?
- Which one creates trust for future phases?
