AI Operations & Executive Support Discovery Questionnaire

Hi Aidan,

Before we build your system, I want to understand how your business actually runs, where the bottlenecks are, and where an AI chief-of-staff style setup can create the most leverage.

This is not meant to be academic. It is here to help me design something genuinely useful around your real workflow.

Answer as briefly or as fully as you like.

1. Business Overview

1. What is the name of your business or brand?
2. How would you describe what you do in one or two sentences?
3. What are your main offers or services right now?
4. Which of those is the main revenue driver today?
5. What kind of clients do you mainly work with? For example, founders, executives, HR teams, enterprise clients, or event organisers.
6. Are you selling mainly to individuals, companies, or both?
7. Which markets or regions matter most to you right now?
8. What would a very successful next 12 months look like for you?

2. Your Workload and Time Drain

9. What does a typical week look like for you?
10. Which parts of your work take the most time or energy?
11. What admin, coordination, or follow-up work do you most dislike doing?
12. Where do things currently get dropped, delayed, or forgotten?
13. What do people regularly need from you that could be handled or prepared by a system first?
14. What kind of work should only ever be done by you personally?

3. Offers, Delivery, and Client Journey

15. What are your core service lines? For example:
- executive coaching
- corporate training days
- keynote speaking
- workshops
- advisory retainers
- team development programmes
16. Walk me through your client journey from first enquiry to delivery.
17. How do new clients usually find you?
18. What happens after someone shows interest?
19. How are proposals, quotes, or bookings currently handled?
20. How do you prepare for a coaching engagement, training day, or speaking event?
21. What follow-up happens after delivery?
22. Where in this journey do you feel the biggest inefficiencies are?

4. Sales and Pipeline

23. How are leads currently tracked?
24. Do you use a CRM? If yes, which one?
25. How do you currently follow up with warm leads?
26. How often do promising conversations go cold because follow-up is inconsistent or delayed?
27. What kind of visibility do you have into your current sales pipeline?
28. If we improved one part of your sales process first, what would create the biggest immediate impact?

5. Content, Speaking, and Personal Brand

29. How important is your personal brand to business growth?
30. Which channels matter most for your visibility right now? For example, LinkedIn, Instagram, email, podcasting, YouTube, or live events.
31. How often are you currently publishing content?
32. What content do you already create, or want to create more consistently?
33. Do you already have talks, notes, recordings, voice notes, newsletters, or transcripts that could be repurposed?
34. What usually stops content from going out consistently?
35. Would you want the system to help with any of the following?
- content ideas
- drafting posts
- repurposing talks into content
- newsletter drafting
- speaker positioning
- case study extraction
- social calendar planning
36. What would a content engine that is working properly actually look like for you?

6. Calendar, Travel, and Executive Support

37. How much of your life is driven by meetings, travel, events, and speaking engagements?
38. What tools do you use for calendar and scheduling?
39. Who manages your diary right now?
40. Do you already have an assistant, EA, ops person, or marketing support?
41. What coordination work around meetings, travel, reminders, prep, and follow-up would you most like to offload?
42. Are there recurring meeting or event types that follow a repeatable pattern?

7. Team and Delegation

43. Is it just you right now, or do you have a team?
44. Who are the key people around you?
45. What responsibilities sit with each person?
46. Where are handoffs currently messy?
47. What do you wish your team was better informed about, without you having to repeat yourself constantly?
48. If an AI system became a real operational layer in the business, who would it need to support besides you?

8. Systems and Tools

49. What tools do you currently use across the business?
50. Which of the following do you already have in place?
- Google Workspace or Microsoft 365
- CRM
- email marketing platform
- calendar booking tool
- project management tool
- note-taking system
- cloud file storage
- analytics or reporting tools
- social scheduling tools
51. Where does important business information currently live?
52. Are your systems fairly organised, or is everything spread across inboxes, notes, WhatsApp, and your head?
53. Which tools absolutely need to stay?
54. Which tools are frustrating, messy, or underused?

9. Communication and Decision-Making Style

55. How do you like support to communicate with you: concise, structured, conversational, challenging, highly proactive?
56. Do you want the system to simply execute, or also challenge your thinking and highlight blind spots?
57. When should the system ask before acting, and when should it take initiative?
58. What kinds of decisions should never be made without you?
59. What would make you trust the system quickly?
60. What would make you lose trust in it quickly?

10. Priorities and High-Value Wins

61. If we could solve only one problem in the first 30 days, what should it be?
62. What would make you say after one month, this is already worth it?
63. Which of these outcomes matter most in the near term?
- more leads
- better follow-up
- smoother operations
- stronger content output
- better client prep and delivery
- less admin
- more strategic clarity
- better team coordination
64. What are the top three areas where you want the AI system to create leverage first?

11. Practical Access and Implementation

65. What communication channel would you prefer to use with your agent? Telegram is usually the fastest to set up.
66. Will the system primarily support just you, or other team members as well?
67. Are there any privacy, confidentiality, or client-data boundaries we need to respect from day one?
68. Are there any systems or accounts you do not want connected?
69. Do you already have a VA, assistant, or ops workflow that this should complement rather than replace?
70. Is there anything important about how you work that I have not asked but should know before designing your system?

Final Question

If this system was working brilliantly for you 90 days from now, what would be noticeably different in your business and your day-to-day life?

Once you send this back, I will turn it into:
- your business snapshot
- highest-value pilot use cases
- recommended system design
- rollout plan for your OpenClaw setup
