## C3. UiPath — "Standard agents vs. Advanced agents"
- **URL:** https://www.uipath.com/blog/ai/technical-tuesday-standard-agents-vs-advanced-agents-whats-different-and-why

### Content
UiPath ($7bn RPA giant) draws a public line:

| | Standard / "Agents 1.0" | Advanced / "Agents 2.0" / "Deep agents" |
|---|---|---|
| Loop | ReAct (think→act→observe) | Plan → execute → replan |
| Best for | Bounded tasks (Q&A, summarise, draft) | Long-horizon multi-system workflows |
| Breaks at | Hundreds of steps, context rot | Designed for hours/days |
| Recovery | Weak — often escalates | Replans, switches approach |

**4 pillars of Advanced agents:**
1. **Explicit planning** (markdown to-do, status tracking, replan on failure)
2. **Sub-agent delegation** (specialised, parallel, task-scoped context)
3. **Skills + heavy system prompts** (codified SOPs, compliance, business logic)
4. **Filesystem as memory** (durable scratchpad, resumable, references not full reloads)

**The "4 signals test"** — if any 2 apply, you need an advanced agent:
- Long-horizon with handoffs
- Inspectable evidence trail required
- Parallelism required
- Context that can't fit a prompt

## C5. Garry Tan — "Skillify"
- **URL:** https://x.com/garrytan/status/2046876981711769720
- **Article:** https://x.com/i/article/2046866228703363072
- **Engagement:** 768k views, 1.5k likes, 142 reposts — viral
- **Posted:** 2026-04-22

### Content
Tan critiques framework-only approaches (LangChain) that give testing tools without an opinionated workflow. His proposal: **"Skillify"** — a 10-step process that converts any agent failure into a permanent, testable, deterministic skill.

### The 10 steps
1. **SKILL.md** — the contract (purpose, triggers, inputs, outputs, guardrails)
2. **Deterministic code** — e.g. `.mjs` scripts. Not LLM. Real code.
3. **Unit tests** — Vitest
4. **Integration tests**
5. **LLM evals** — daily, LLM-as-judge
6. **Resolver trigger** in AGENTS.md — *when* the skill should fire
7. **Resolver eval** — does the trigger correctly fire?
8. **Check-resolvable + DRY audit** — no skill duplication, no "dark skills"
9. **E2E smoke tests**
10. **Brain filing rules** — where artefacts live

Verb form: **"skillify it."** Prototype in chat → say "skillify it" → agent builds the full pipeline.

### Worked examples from the post
- **Failure:** Agent hit live calendar APIs for old data instead of local grep. **Fix:** `calendar-recall` skill + script.
- **Failure:** Timezone math error ("28 minutes" vs 88). **Fix:** `context-now` skill enforcing a precise deterministic script.

### Why this matters more than most
We already have B2 (his "durable agent" rule) embedded in AGENTS.md. **C5 is the full operational pipeline behind that rule.** We've been doing step 1 (SKILL.md) but not 2-10. That gap is silently degrading our skills over time — they're not deterministic, not unit-tested, not eval'd, drift detection isn't running.

This is also the **implementation methodology behind UiPath's C3 pillar 3 (skills + heavy prompts).** UiPath says "you need skills"; Tan tells you how to build/maintain them properly.

### Self-application: today's bookmark-recall failure
Jonny caught me missing prior bookmark batches earlier this session. Under Skillify, that exact failure becomes a `bookmark-recall` skill:
- **Step 1 (SKILL.md):** triggers on any consultancy/content/deployment work, scans INDEX-by-trigger.md
- **Step 2 (deterministic code):** a script that confirms INDEX read happened before the response was generated
- **Step 3-4 (tests):** known-positive ("prospect onboarding" should pull C3, C1) and known-negative scenarios
- **Step 5 (LLM eval):** weekly judge run on "did I correctly surface bookmarks at trigger moments"
- **Step 6 (resolver trigger):** the BOOKMARKS-FIRST rule we just added to AGENTS.md
- **Steps 7-10:** ongoing

We did Layer 1 + 2 informally tonight. Skillify is the formal version. Worth doing.

### Why this matters for consultancy
1. **Compounding-value sales pitch:** *"Every failure your agent makes becomes a permanent improvement. By month 6, your agent is uniquely tuned to your business."* That's lock-in without SaaS lock-in.
2. **Skillify becomes Phase 3 of the engagement.** Phase 1: Map workflows (Khairallah C1). Phase 2: Build (UiPath C3 4 pillars). **Phase 3: Skillify ongoing failures (Tan C5).** Clean three-phase product roadmap.
3. **YC CEO's brand on the methodology** = same enterprise air-cover as UiPath. Tan's audience = our exact ICP (founders running SMEs).
4. **Auditable failure log = evidence trail** = one of UiPath's 4 signals = matters for regulated industries (Dano).

- Spring Boot service grew 119 → 950 lines in 5 autonomous cycles

**The pattern:** *Give an AI a metric and let it self-improve until it wins.* Started in ML training. Generalised to almost anything measurable.

## C12. rohit4verse — "Compounding context only compounds if you maintain it"
- **URL:** https://x.com/rohit4verse/status/2046992964388626596

*"One thing this article skipped. Compounding context only compounds if you maintain it.*

*I have watched three separate 'AI knowledge bases' turn into garbage heaps in under six months. No decay function, so stale facts outvote fresh ones. No contradiction handling, so the model pulls an outdated answer from 2024 and a correct one from 2026 into the same response and picks whichever one ranks higher.*

*The teams winning at this spend as much engineering on forgetting as they do on remembering.*
*Build the knowledge base in week one. Build the decay function in week two. Skip the second one and the first one becomes a liability."*

### Why this is the highest-leverage bookmark of the batch
Every other bookmark in the batch (C4 Membase, C5 Skillify, C8 Autoresearch, C9 Memory-Layered Context) talks about *adding* to the knowledge base. C12 names the *drain*. Without a drain, the system overflows.

**The 4-phase consultancy roadmap (Map → Build → Skillify → Autoresearch) all add. None subtract.** By month 12 of any client engagement, without explicit decay, the knowledge base degrades. That degradation undermines exactly the "compounding value" pitch we're selling.

This bookmark forces a fifth phase: **Maintain.**
